General Assembly

Model United Nations Conference

1. Introduction

The United Nations General Assembly (GA) committee in a Model UN simulates the plenary body of the UN, where all Member States debate overarching global issues and adopt non-binding resolutions.

2. Mandate & Objectives

  • Deliberation: Provide a forum for multilateral discussion on international issues.
  • Norm-Setting: Adopt declarations and resolutions guiding international norms.
  • Budgetary Oversight: Approve the UN budget and assess financial contributions.
  • Coordination: Promote cooperation across UN organs and agencies.

3. Committee Structure

  • Membership: All 193 UN Member States with equal voting rights.
  • Dais: President (elected annually), Vice-Presidents, and Rapporteur.

4. Typical Session Flow

  1. Opening Plenary: General debate and agenda adoption.
  2. Main Committees: Six thematic committees meet concurrently.
  3. Caucusing: Informal negotiations and bloc formation.
  4. Drafting Resolutions: Working papers evolve into draft resolutions.
  5. Amendments & Voting: Debate and vote on amendments and resolutions.
  6. Closing Plenary: Adoption of all resolutions and closing remarks.

5. Example Agenda Topics

  • Promoting Sustainable Development in Developing Countries
  • Ensuring Global Food Security
  • Addressing Climate Change and Disaster Risk Reduction
  • Strengthening International Cooperation on Cybersecurity

6. Draft Resolution Components

  • Preambulatory Clauses: Reference GA resolutions, UN Charter, and international law.
  • Operative Clauses: Recommendations, timelines, and calls for action.

7. Rules of Procedure

  • Speakers’ List
  • Points & Motions
  • Voting Procedures: Simple majority for resolutions; two-thirds for important questions.

8. Delegate Preparation & Strategy

  • Research your country’s position on global issues.
  • Prepare a comprehensive position paper.
  • Join regional and thematic blocs.
  • Use UN reports, treaties, and data for evidence-based proposals.

9. Expected Outcomes

  • Non-binding resolutions reflecting global consensus.
  • Increased awareness of multilateral diplomacy.
  • Strengthened skills in negotiation and policy formulation.

10. Conclusion

Participation in the General Assembly committee offers delegates the chance to engage in broad multilateral discussions and contribute to global norm-setting.
